You have two empty jars and a lake. The jars have some volume. You can fill each of jars from the lake, pour from one jar to other or pour out to lake. You should measure some volume of water in any jar. Each action is describe as string of two symbols: From and where. The jars are marked as 1 and 2, the lake -- 0. So if you want to fill first jar, then it's "01". Pour water from second to first -- "21". Pour out water from first jar -- "10". When you fill or pour out a jar, it can be done only with whole water in the jar. If you pour water from one jar to another, you pour only sufficient volume to fill a full jar.

The function got three arguments: The volume of first jar, the volume of second jar and the goal. All arguments are positive integer (number > 0). You should return a list with action's sequence. It must contain minimal possible quantity of steps

Input: The volume of first jar, the volume of second jar and the goal. Positive integers.

Output: A sequence of steps. A list with strings.

Example: 

checkio(5, 7, 6) == ["01", "12", "01", "12", "20", "12", "20", "12", "01", "12"]
checkio(3, 4, 1) == ["02", "21"]
